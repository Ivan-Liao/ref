6. Gross Profit Margin: The revenue remaining after deducting the cost of goods sold (COGS), expressed as a percentage.
7. Inventory Turnover: The number of times a company sells and replaces its stock of goods over a given period.
8. Return on Ad Spend (ROAS): The amount of revenue earned for every dollar spent on a marketing campaign.
9. Net Promoter Score (NPS): A metric measuring customer loyalty and the likelihood of customers recommending the business to others.
10. Customer Retention Rate: The percentage of customers a company retains over a specific period.

# Healthcare (Hospitals & Providers)
1. Patient Wait Time: The average amount of time a patient spends waiting to see a provider from the moment they arrive.
2. Average Length of Stay (ALOS): The average number of days patients spend in the hospital for a particular treatment or overall.
3. Patient Satisfaction Score: Metrics derived from surveys (like HCAHPS) evaluating a patient's experience with the facility and staff.
4. Hospital Readmission Rate: The percentage of patients who return to the hospital within a specified period (often 30 days) after being discharged.
5. Bed Occupancy Rate: The percentage of available hospital beds that are actively in use by patients.
6. Cost Per Patient: The average expense incurred by the facility to treat a single patient.
7. Patient-to-Staff Ratio: The number of patients assigned to a single nurse or healthcare provider at any given time.
8. ER Door-to-Doctor Time: The duration between a patient's arrival at the emergency room and their initial assessment by a physician.
9. Treatment Success Rate: The percentage of patients who experience positive health outcomes following a specific procedure or treatment plan.
10. Claim Denial Rate: The percentage of medical claims submitted to insurance that are rejected or denied.

# Real Estate
1. Days on Market (DOM): The average number of days a property is listed actively before an offer is accepted.
2. Sales Volume: The total dollar value of all real estate transactions closed within a specific period.
3. Average Commission per Sale: The typical gross revenue earned by an agent or brokerage on a single closed transaction.
4. Lead-to-Listing Conversion Rate: The percentage of prospective seller leads that result in a signed listing agreement.
5. Listing-to-Sale Conversion Rate: The percentage of active listings that successfully close as opposed to expiring or being withdrawn.
6. Year-Over-Year (YOY) Growth: The comparison of current performance metrics (like revenue or sales volume) against the same period in the previous year.
7. Gross Yield: The annual rental income of a property divided by its purchase price or market value, used primarily in property management and investing.
8. Vacancy Rate: The percentage of available rental units that are unoccupied at a given time.
9. Client Acquisition Cost: The marketing and advertising expenses divided by the number of new clients secured.
10. Active Listings Inventory: The total number of properties currently available for sale in a specific market or brokerage.

# Health Insurance
Medical Loss Ratio (MLR): The percentage of premium revenues spent on clinical services and quality improvement.

Claims Denial Rate: The percentage of submitted claims that the insurance company refuses to pay.

Average Cost Per Claim: The total payout for claims divided by the number of approved claims.

Turnaround Time for Claims Processing: The average time it takes the company to resolve and payout a claim from the moment it is submitted.

Member Retention Rate: The percentage of policyholders who renew their insurance plans at the end of their coverage term.

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): The total expense of acquiring a new policyholder, including marketing and broker commissions.

Operating Expense Ratio: The cost of running the insurance business (administrative, marketing, salaries) compared to the premiums earned.

Provider Network Growth: The rate at which new doctors, hospitals, and clinics are added to the insurer's approved network.

Premium Revenue Growth: The increase or decrease in money collected from policyholders over a set time frame.

Member Satisfaction Score: Survey-based metrics tracking how happy policyholders are with their coverage, customer service, and portal usage.

Banking (Retail & Commercial)
Net Interest Margin (NIM): The difference between the interest income generated by banks and the amount of interest paid out to their lenders, relative to the amount of their interest-earning assets.

Return on Assets (ROA): An indicator of how profitable a bank is relative to its total assets.

Return on Equity (ROE): A measure of financial performance calculated by dividing net income by shareholders' equity.

Non-Performing Loan (NPL) Ratio: The percentage of total loans that are in default or close to being in default.

Loan-to-Deposit Ratio (LDR): A metric used to assess a bank's liquidity by comparing its total loans to its total deposits.

Cost-to-Income Ratio: A measure of operational efficiency, calculated by dividing operating expenses by operating income.

Customer Acquisition Cost: The marketing and promotional costs associated with getting a customer to open a new account or take out a loan.

Churn Rate: The rate at which customers close their accounts or stop doing business with the bank.

Tier 1 Capital Ratio: A comparison of a bank's core equity capital to its total risk-weighted assets, used to measure financial strength.

Average Revenue Per User (ARPU): The average amount of money generated from a single customer across all banking products (accounts, loans, credit cards).

Finance (Corporate & Investment)
EBITDA: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization; a measure of a company's overall financial performance.

Working Capital Ratio: Also known as the current ratio, it measures a company's ability to pay its short-term obligations with its current assets.

Debt-to-Equity Ratio: A metric calculating the weight of total debt and financial liabilities against total shareholders' equity.

Return on Investment (ROI): A performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency or profitability of an investment.

Operating Cash Flow: The amount of cash generated by a company's normal business operations.

Gross Profit Margin: The percentage of revenue left after deducting the costs of producing the goods sold.

Net Profit Margin: The percentage of revenue remaining after all operating expenses, interest, and taxes have been deducted.

Quick Ratio: A measure of a company's ability to meet its short-term obligations using its most liquid assets (excluding inventory).

Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC): The average rate that a company pays to finance its assets, calculated by averaging the cost of all capital sources.

Free Cash Flow (FCF): The cash a company generates after accounting for cash outflows to support operations and maintain its capital assets.

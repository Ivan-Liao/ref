1. Ablation  
   1. Destruction and removal of tissue using heat or freezing temperatures.   
2. Dosimetry  
   1. Measurement, calculation, and assessment of absorbed dose of ionizing radiation.  
   2. Dosimeters are devices like rings or badges   
3. Fibroblast  
   1. The most common type of cell found in connective tissue throughout the human body. They are primarily responsible for synthesizing collagen and the extracellular matrix, which provide the structural framework (stroma) for tissues and play a critical role in wound healing  
4. PET (positron emission tomography)  
   1. Low level radiation imaging
